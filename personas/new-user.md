# Persona: New User (first run)

**Who:** Just signed up. Has never seen this app. Mildly tech-comfortable but impatient and
literal — reads labels at face value, doesn't hunt through menus.

**Mindset:** "I heard this helps me keep track of stuff around my house. Let me try to add
my first thing and see if it does anything useful." Easily put off by empty screens that
don't tell me what to do next.

**Goal:** From a cold start, add your first asset (e.g. a kitchen appliance) and set a
reminder on it (e.g. replace the filter in 3 months). Reach the moment where the app feels
like it's actually doing something for you.

**What to scrutinize (friction):**
- The empty/first-run state — does it tell me what to do, or dump me into a blank screen?
- Is the primary "add" action obvious within ~5 seconds?
- Does the add flow ask for things I don't know or care about yet?
- After adding, is it clear what happened and what to do next?

**Constraints / honest-eyes:**
- No prior knowledge of where anything is. Discover everything from the screen.
- If the path isn't obvious, log the confusion before exploring — don't pretend it was easy.
